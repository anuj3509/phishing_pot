                        <td valign="top" class="mcnTextContent" style="padding: 0px 18px 9px;font-family: Roboto, &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;font-size: 16px;font-style: normal;font-weight: normal;line-height: 150%;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;word-break: break-word;color: #292929;text-align: left;">

                            <h1 class="null" style="text-align: left;display: block;margin: 0;padding: 0;color: #292929;font-family: Georgia, Times, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;font-style: normal;font-weight: bold;line-height: 200%;letter-spacing: 1px;"><span style="color:#A52A2A"><span class="mc-toc-title"><span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif">On our radar</span></span></span></span></h1>

<p style="font-family: Roboto, &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;font-size: 16px;font-style: normal;font-weight: normal;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #292929;text-align: left;"><span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>Outcry over Bucha</strong></span></span><br>
&nbsp;<br>
The massacre of what authorities <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=8c1a5baac5&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">say</a> is at least 320 civilians in Bucha, a small city northwest of Kyiv, has <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=2c64aa48be&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">captured</a> <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=953f14135c&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">global attention</a>. Between <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=2df661f653&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">150 and 300</a> bodies were buried in a mass grave in the city while others were left <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=a24b569dbd&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">scattered</a> along a street for weeks. <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=8643a178b9&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Hundreds</a> were also killed in other towns outside of Kyiv, such as Borodianka and Hostomel, while an alleged Russian missile strike on a railway station in the southeastern city of Kramatorsk on 8 April&nbsp;<a data-remove-tab-index="true" data-sk="tooltip_parent" data-stringify-link="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2022/apr/08/kramatorsk-train-station-ukraine-russia-rockets" delay="150" href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=1e0d649c99&e=36a804d8a7" rel="noopener noreferrer" tabindex="-1" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">killed at least 50 civilians, including several children</a>. Across the country, <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=b0c5a8be32&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Ukrainian</a> prosecutors are investigating <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=f6d83be751&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">5,000</a> potential war crimes. But it was images of the bodies in Bucha – revealed after Russian troops withdrew from areas outside the Ukrainian capital last weekend – that spurred the international community to action: The US has <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=01e75edae4&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">imposed</a> a fresh round of sanctions, with the UK and <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=651d79ad6e&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">EU</a> following suit; and the UN General Assembly took the rare step of <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=7d0feb2c51&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">voting</a> to suspend Russia from the organisation’s leading human rights body. As justified as they are, the outrage and calls for accountability – which may ultimately be <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=1982a9bc57&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">difficult</a> to enforce – stand in stark contrast to the lack of attention or effort to prevent atrocities and hold perpetrators accountable elsewhere in the world, be it in Mali (see below), Yemen, Syria, Myanmar, or a host of other countries.<br>
&nbsp;<br>
<span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>Silence over Moura</strong></span></span><br>
&nbsp;<br>
As the Bucha killings triggered global outcry, an atrocity involving Russian forces in the central Malian town of Moura garnered far less attention. Mercenaries from the Kremlin-linked Wagner Group are accused of executing between 300-500 people while conducting an anti-jihadist offensive alongside the Malian army. <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=ac78586006&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Rights groups say</a> the massacre –&nbsp;which started on 27 March – is the worst single atrocity reported in a decade-long armed conflict. Mali’s ruling junta has opened a <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=fca05a2a99&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">military tribunal</a> to probe the incident but is <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=a9c876c1d9&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">preventing</a> the UN from visiting Moura to conduct an independent investigation. The Malian army <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=a2ce1e5959&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">claims</a> 203 “terrorists” were killed during the operation, but reports suggest the majority of the casualties were civilians from the pastoralist Fulani group – long stigmatised after its members joined local jihadists in large numbers. Wagner Group fighters arrived in Mali late last year, prompting France and allied European countries to withdraw their anti-jihadist forces. Rights abuses have soared ever since. Read <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=f58f1319ad&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">our recent briefing on the situation</a> for more.<br>
&nbsp;<br>
<span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>A tentative ceasefire holds (for now) in Yemen</strong></span></span><br>
&nbsp;<br>
Yemen is one week into a nationwide ceasefire that is supposed to last (an extendable) two months. While <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=076a9895c6&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">both sides accused</a> the other of violations almost right from the jump, the UN-negotiated deal does appear to be holding for now. The <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=066668a679&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">UN is hoping</a> that the truce, which coincided with the start of the Muslim holy month of Ramadan, will be an opening for talks and the eventual end of a seven-year war that has brought on death, economic collapse, and <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=a451ba9344&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">widespread hunger</a> – a crisis that may soon become an even bigger problem as <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=8921b3015b&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">bread prices rise</a> thanks to the war in Ukraine. The ceasefire is also supposed to allow fuel ships into the northern port of <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=704e43a07b&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Hodeidah</a>, which could provide some relief to civilians dealing with increasing food and transport costs caused by shortages. The deal also says some civilian flights will be let in and out of the Sana’a airport for the first time in years, and negotiations will begin about opening roads into the <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=e97e1c7208&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">long besieged city of Taiz</a>. Various agreements have come and gone over the course of the conflict: Could this be the one that sticks?&nbsp;<br>
&nbsp;<br>
<span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>Alarm bells for the dammed Mekong</strong></span></span><br>
&nbsp;<br>
Climate disasters and “unsustainable development” are threatening food security for tens of millions who depend on the Mekong River, the head of the intergovernmental Mekong River Commission warns. “Given the state of the Mekong today, we just cannot relax. Our feet should be on fire, and we need to act to survive,” Anoulak Kittikhoun said in a speech on <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=f7b6cc5be2&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">5 April</a>. It’s a relatively blunt message from a usually taciturn organisation set up to promote cooperation along the Mekong system, which winds through parts of China, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam. The Mekong powers agriculture and fisheries across the region, but it’s struggling. Wet-season water flows have halved, and the monsoon-driven season itself has even shrunk by a month. Wetlands are disappearing, there’s less sediment to supercharge floodplain agriculture, and rising salinity is damaging rice crops. Climate change is a factor, but human-caused development – like an extensive system of hydropower dams, particularly in Laos and China – has a “profound” impact on wet-season droughts, a <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=d217d53dbc&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">March report</a> from the US-based Stimson Center think tank found. While scenarios for the Mekong are <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=4e5f188025&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">alarming</a>, there’s also a degree of measured hope in uncertainty: It’s too soon to say if low water levels are a new normal, researchers said. Regardless, better cooperation on managing upstream flows will lessen downstream suffering.<br>
&nbsp;<br>
<span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>Colonial conservation in DR Congo</strong></span></span><br>
&nbsp;<br>
Foreign-funded guards and soldiers protecting the Democratic Republic of Congo’s Kahuzi-Biega National Park are accused of violently expelling Indigenous Batwa communities in <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=178c66cbfb&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">a new report</a> from the London-based Minority Rights Group (MRG). Batwa were expelled from their ancestral homelands in the 1970s when the park was created but rebuilt their villages in late 2018 after promises of resettlement and reparations were broken. Park guards and Congolese troops then conducted a three-year expulsion campaign that allegedly saw villages burnt, women gang-raped, and at least 20 people killed. The park’s international partners –&nbsp;which include the German and US governments – were informed about the rights violations but continued their funding, according to MRG. The report <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=558da234e2&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">describes</a> the expulsions as emblematic of a colonial conservation model that views parks as “unpeopled” spaces to be enjoyed by foreign tourists and conservationists, at the expense of Indigenous inhabitants.<br>

<span style="font-size:18px"><span style="font-family:georgia,times,times new roman,serif"><strong>Ukraine, the climate crisis, and fuel taxes in Peru</strong></span></span><br>

While President Pedro Castillo’s embattled government announced the <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=44fe3ee102&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">suspension of fuel taxes</a> in an attempt to reduce the unrest, authors of a new <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=a1ba311253&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">report</a> published in Geneva warned it was “<a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=77615778ee&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">now or never</a>” to make deep and immediate emissions cuts to avoid a global climate emergency. In an interview republished this week by The New Humanitarian, a <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=d748923929&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">lead author of the food section of one of the recent IPCC reports</a> urged more inclusive adaptation strategies to confront the global food crisis, including through the use of <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=115f1c2f51&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Indigenous knowledge</a>. For a related solutions lens, check out Anthony Wallace’s in-depth report this week exploring how farmers in the <a href="https://thenewhumanitarian.us12.list-manage.com/track/click?u=31c0c755a8105c17c23d89842&id=78d23c86c3&e=36a804d8a7" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #a03c50;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: none;">Navajo Nation</a> are embracing sustainable agriculture to overcome health disparities and racial neglect.</p>
